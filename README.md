Unemployment Analysis with Python 📊
This repository contains a comprehensive analysis of the unemployment rate in India, focusing on the period between 2019 and 2020. This project was completed as part of the CodeAlpha Data Science Internship (Task 2).

📋 Project Objective
The goal of this project is to analyze the unemployment trends in India, specifically investigating the socio-economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on the labor market across different states and regions (Urban vs. Rural).

🗂️ Dataset Description
The dataset includes the following key features:

Region: The state in India.

Date: The month and year of the record.

Estimated Unemployment Rate (%): The percentage of the labor force that is unemployed.

Estimated Employed: The count of people currently employed.

Estimated Labour Participation Rate (%): The percentage of the working-age population that is either working or actively looking for work.

Area: Categorized as Rural or Urban.

🛠️ Tech Stack
Language: Python 3.x

Libraries:

Pandas: Data cleaning and manipulation.

Matplotlib & Seaborn: Static data visualization.

Plotly: Interactive visualizations.

Datetime: Handling time-series data.

🚀 Key Insights from Analysis
COVID-19 Impact: National unemployment jumped from an average of 9.5% (Pre-Lockdown) to over 17.8% (During Lockdown).

Urban vs. Rural: Urban areas consistently showed higher unemployment rates and were more sensitive to economic shutdowns than rural agricultural areas.

Regional Extremes: States like Tripura, Haryana, and Jharkhand recorded the highest average unemployment rates. During the peak of the lockdown (April 2020), some regions saw unemployment rates spike as high as 76%.

Labor Participation: There is a strong negative correlation between the unemployment rate and the labor participation rate, indicating that as jobs disappeared, people also stopped looking for work.

📊 Visualizations Included
Time-Series Line Charts: Tracking the rise of unemployment throughout 2020.

Boxplots: Comparing the distribution of unemployment in Urban vs. Rural sectors.

Bar Charts: Identifying the top 10 most affected states.

Correlation Heatmaps: Understanding the relationship between employment metrics.
